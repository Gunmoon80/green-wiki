2. Syllable Structure

  • Preferred syllable structure is (C)V(C), where C = consonant, V = vowel.
  • Clusters of consonants are resolved by inserting epenthetic vowels ("a" or "e") as necessary.
  • Words ending in a consonant are suffixed with a schwa-like "e" ([ə]) for ease of pronunciation.

Example: dbu ("cat" shifted) is pronounced [daˈbu].

3. Word Formation and Shifting

  1. For each English word, shift every letter one step forward in the alphabet (A→B, ..., Z→A).
  2. Render the shifted word as a single phonological unit, not as a sequence of letters.
  3. If the resulting word is not pronounceable according to the syllable rules, insert vowels as needed.

4. Prosody and Stress

  • Primary stress is always placed on the first syllable of each word.
  • Sentences are spoken with a neutral or slightly rising intonation at the end, unless otherwise indicated by punctuation.
  • Pauses between words are brief and regular.

5. Grammatical Conventions

  • No inflection for tense, number, or case. All grammatical information is carried by word order and context.
  • Questions are indicated solely by a rising intonation at the end of the sentence.
  • Proper nouns retain capitalization but follow the same shifting and pronunciation rules.

6. Orthographic Notes

  • All punctuation is preserved from the source language.
  • Numbers are not shifted and are pronounced as in English.
  • Non-alphabetic characters (e.g., hyphens, apostrophes) are retained and pronounced as pauses.

8. Summary of Procedure

  1. Shift each letter of the source word forward by one in the English alphabet (A→B, ..., Z→A).
  2. Segment the shifted word into pronounceable syllables, inserting vowels as required.
  3. Apply the pronunciation rules for vowels and consonants as described above.
  4. Maintain primary stress on the first syllable of each word.
  5. Observe neutral or rising intonation for statements and questions, respectively.

9. Appendix: Special Cases

  • If the shifted form results in ambiguous pronunciation, default to the closest CV or VC syllable structure.
  • For loanwords or technical terms, shift as normal and adapt to the syllable and pronunciation rules.
  • For acronyms, shift each letter and pronounce as a sequence of syllables.

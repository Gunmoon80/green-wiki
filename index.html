greenlish is a constructed language (conlang) and simple substitution cipher in which each letter of the English alphabet is shifted forward by one position. For example, "A" becomes "B", "B" becomes "C", and "Z" wraps around to "A". This playful language is often used in games, puzzles, and as an introduction to cryptography.

History

greenlish origins come from a discord server named Bumcord (formerly Mooncord), in which a member named "Gunmoon" decided to copy purplish and created this wiki just for you. It is inspired by the Caesar cipher, one of the oldest and simplest known encryption techniques. greenlish is sometimes used in online communities and chat platforms for fun or to obscure messages, as seen in the Discord conversation example.

Alphabet and Encoding

In greenlish, every letter is replaced by the previous letter in the English alphabet. If the letter is "A", it cycles back to "Z". The same rule applies to lowercase letters. Non-letter characters (such as spaces and punctuation) are not changed.

Typing and Orthography

The written form of greenlish is governed by strict substitution and orthographic conventions to ensure clarity and consistency across digital and handwritten media. This section details the rules and best practices for typing, formatting, and displaying greenlish text.

Letter Substitution

  • Each letter in the original English text is replaced by the next letter in the Latin alphabet (A‚ÜíZ, ..., Z‚ÜíY; a‚Üíz, ..., z‚Üíy).
  • Non-alphabetic characters (spaces, punctuation, numbers, symbols) are preserved and appear unchanged in the output.

Capitalization

  • Capitalization is preserved. For example, "Hello" becomes "Gdkkn".
  • All-caps text remains all-caps after shifting: "WARNING" ‚Üí "VZQMHMF".
  • Title case and sentence case are also preserved in the shifted output.

Punctuation and Symbols

  • Punctuation marks (periods, commas, exclamation points, question marks, etc.) are not shifted and retain their original positions.
  • Quotation marks, parentheses, dashes, and other symbols are not altered.
  • Emojis and special Unicode symbols are preserved as-is.

Numbers

  • Numerals (0-9) are not shifted and appear unchanged.
  • Ordinal and cardinal numbers retain their format: "Room 101" ‚Üí "Qnnl 101".

Formatting in Digital Communication

  • Line breaks, tabs, and spacing are retained.
  • Markdown, HTML, or BBCode formatting tags are not shifted and should be applied after encoding.
  • For code blocks or inline code, encode only the text, not the markup syntax.

Accessibility and Readability

  • For accessibility, it is recommended to provide the original (unshifted) text as a tooltip or in parentheses for screen readers or those unfamiliar with greenlish.
  • When using greenlish in educational or puzzle contexts, display both original and shifted text side by side for clarity.

Common Mistakes

  • Do not shift non-letter characters.
  • Do not change the case of letters unless the original text does.
  • Do not shift letters in code, tags, or formatting syntax.

Best Practices

  • When sharing greenlish text, consider providing a key or reference for those unfamiliar with the system.
  • For puzzles or games, clarify whether answers should be in greenlish or plain English.
  • When using greenlish in a collaborative document, use consistent formatting for readability.

Usage

greenlish is not used for formal communication but is popular among hobbyists, in online chats, and as an educational tool in cryptography. Its simplicity makes it accessible for beginners interested in learning about ciphers and constructed languages.

For a comprehensive and technical guide to the spoken system, see greenlish: Spoken System Reference.
